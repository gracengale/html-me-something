An Introduction

This is my dad, John.

He’s the Atticus to my Scout, and we’ve always been a great team.

During my sophomore year of college, I was known as the “stray cat of the MSU theatre department”. Due to some tough circumstances, I spent most of the year couch-surfing between my friends’ houses. Dad knew that was taking a toll on me, so he spent that time secretly fundraising. In May, when I had officially survived the school year, he surprised me with a trip to Europe to cheer me up.

Some things you should know about John Minnis:

  1. He is obsessed with history - specifically genealogy. Honestly, the people that invented Ancestry.com should really just let him run it.
  2. He was born with the travel bug, which is apparently hereditary. We are both passionate about world history and adventure.
  3. He is a gigantic goofball and an even bigger klutz - always getting into hilarious predicaments. It's probably from all of the I Love Lucy he watches.

The Search

We landed in Edinburgh on the fourth day with one goal in mind.

You see, when I was growing up we had a hand-drawn picture of the house my Scottish ancestors lived in framed in our house. My dad made it his goal to see that house in person. All he had to go on, though, was a dinky little stick- figure map of like three roads put together.

After some research, he found out that the house was called the High Alderstocks (which is absolutely insane considering the street I grew up on was Aldergate). Anyway, dad eventually scrounged together some info. We now knew that the house was in Strathaven [STRAY-VEN], Lanarkshire, Scotland, and that the current owners were Dugie and Margaret Turner.

The Alderstocks

We got to the charming little Scottish town of Strathaven, and asked the locals working in the bakery for 1) directions because we didn’t have internet abroad, and 2) shortbread! After three hours of driving (and getting lost) around rainy hills, we found it.

Margaret & Dugie showed us around, made us tea, and showed us pictures of my great-great-great grandmother Agnes Pate.

Agnes had 16 cats that were all named after tennis players. They told us stories that had circled the locals about our family and told us where to find their graves.

The Cemetery

So later, high up on a cemetery hill, we searched for the graves of Peter and Mary Clelland. Unfortunately, all of the graves were so old that over half were no longer decipherable, and the others were broken. So we decided to leave. On the way out, dad stopped to tie his shoe. While he was kneeling, he happened to look at the grave next to him and - I kid you not - saw the word “ALDERSTOCKS”. We could just make out the name Clelland, and my dad started to cry.

Thoughts/Conclusion

Family history is more than stories. It explains why we are who we are. It tells us where we come from and what our ancestors went through to get us here. My dad taught me that, and I will never forget that historical experience we shared. I hope these memories are passed down to my descendants, and even more, I hope that they cherish them as much as John Minnis would.
